## The industry landscape

AI automation consulting ranges wildly depending on who you work with:

- **Freelancers / solo consultants:** $2,000–$10,000 for a project. Pros: affordable, direct communication. Cons: limited capacity, variable quality, may lack systems expertise.

- **Mid-tier agencies (like us):** $8,000–$25,000 for a build, plus $1,500–$4,000/month retainers. Pros: custom-built solutions, dedicated support, proven processes. Cons: higher upfront investment.

- **Enterprise consulting firms:** $50,000–$500,000+ for large-scale implementations. Pros: deep bench, enterprise-grade. Cons: slow timelines, massive overhead, you're paying for the brand name.

- **DIY with tools (Zapier, Make.com):** $20–$600/month in subscription costs, plus your time. Pros: cheapest entry point. Cons: limited to rule-based automation, no AI intelligence, maintenance burden falls on you.

## What drives the cost up?

Here are the factors that increase project cost:

- **Number of tools to integrate.** Connecting 3 tools is simpler than connecting 12. Each integration requires mapping data flows, handling edge cases, and testing.

- **Workflow complexity.** A linear process (A → B → C) is cheaper to automate than a branching process with exceptions, approvals, and conditional logic.

- **Data quality.** If your existing data is messy, inconsistent, or siloed, we may need to do cleanup and normalization work before automation can begin.

- **Compliance and security requirements.** Industries like healthcare, finance, and legal may require additional security measures, audit trails, and access controls.

- **Team size and training.** Larger teams need more onboarding, documentation, and change management support.

## The DIY vs. agency tradeoff

Can you build automation yourself with Zapier or Make.com? Absolutely — for simple workflows. Here's the honest comparison:

**DIY approach:**
- Cost: $50–$600/month in tools + your time (or a team member's time)
- Timeline: Ongoing, incremental
- Result: Rule-based automations that handle simple, predictable tasks
- Hidden cost: Your time. If you or a senior team member is spending 5 hours a week building and maintaining automations, that's $15,000–$25,000/year in opportunity cost

**Agency approach:**
- Cost: $8,000–$25,000 upfront + $1,500–$4,000/month
- Timeline: 4–8 weeks to deployment
- Result: Intelligent, agentic system that handles complex workflows with AI
- ROI: Most clients save 20+ hours/week, recouping the investment in 2–3 months

The DIY approach makes sense if your needs are simple and your workflows don't require judgment. The agency approach makes sense if you want to automate complex operations and you'd rather invest in a system that works than spend your time becoming an automation expert.

## How to evaluate ROI before you invest

Before spending a dollar, you should be able to answer:

1. **How many hours per week does your team spend on the tasks you want to automate?** Multiply by your average hourly cost. That's your monthly savings potential.

2. **What's the error rate on manual processes?** Missed follow-ups, late invoices, wrong data entry — each error has a cost. Automation reduces these to near zero.

3. **What's the opportunity cost?** What could your team accomplish if they weren't buried in admin? Revenue growth, client relationships, strategic initiatives — these compound over time.

4. **What's the payback period?** Take the project cost, divide by monthly savings. Most businesses land at 2–4 months.

If the math works, the investment is a no-brainer. If it doesn't, a good consultant will tell you that honestly — which is exactly what we do in our free audit.
